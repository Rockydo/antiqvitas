# M5 Via Popilia corridor

The M5 road ledger adds `rimini = ravenna` and `ravenna = rovigo` as contested,
high-level installed-map proxies for the Via Popilia corridor. The Italian
Ministry of Culture's Roman-Rimini catalogue identifies the 132 BCE road as
running from Rimini through Ravenna and Adria toward Altino.

The installed AD 1 map contains Roman-controlled Rimini, Ravenna, and Rovigo,
but lacks Adria, Altino, and the intermediate stations. These links therefore
represent only the documented regional trajectory; they do not assert an exact
surveyed trace, make Rovigo identical to Adria, or imply a pre-period Venice.

`tools/generate_start_mirror.py` validates both endpoints and renders the
locally verified vanilla bare endpoint contract. This adds connectivity only and
does not resolve the separate runtime-effective RGO/trade-flow blocker.

Sources: ANTIQVITAS master plan sections 8.1 and 12.2; Stanford ORBIS route
sanity; Italian Ministry of Culture, ["Città romana di Ariminum /
Rimini"](https://catalogo.cultura.gov.it/detail/ibc/ArchaeologicalProperty/261525).
