# M5 Oc Eo growth market

## Evidence

UNESCO's Cambodia-submitted Tentative List describes Angkor Borei as an
important early complex-polity centre, occupied from the first millennium BCE
into the late sixth century CE. It also records a long canal connecting it with
other Funan centres and the Oc Eo region. That is sufficient evidence for a
small early regional exchange node, but it does not establish the exact
archaeological footprint, size, political status, or built harbour of Oc Eo in
AD 1.

## Engine representation

The plan lists Oc Eo as a Southeast Asian growth hub. The installed
`angkor_borei` location is therefore a deliberately contested near-region
proxy. It becomes M5's 42nd market and a town-tier urban node. The existing
later-peak `angkor` anchor is retained separately. No harbour building,
independent Funan tag, or precise city footprint is inferred from this entry.

## Verification

The generated start manager reports 42 M5 markets, 42 urban nodes, and 33
M5/M7 buildings. `make validate` passed. Enabled-mod `make smoke` reached the
menu and found zero new `error.log` lines against the accepted baseline.
