# M5 Laurion decline audit

## Evidence

The plan marks Laurion as a fading source rather than an AD 1 mine expansion.
The *Princeton Encyclopedia of Classical Sites* identifies the Laurion district
as Attica's southeast lead-silver mining landscape. It reports that the
Classical period was its high point and that, by Strabo's time, underground
mining had ceased and workers were reworking slag heaps. This supports a
declined, secondary activity at the campaign boundary; it does not establish a
January AD 1 output, an operating mine surface, or a discrete production site.

## Engine disposition

No live silver RGO is added. The installed map has no `laurion`, `lavrion`, or
`sounion` location key; its broader `athens` location retains its existing
marble raw material. Turning that city-scale key into a productive silver mine
would contradict the source's decline evidence and create a false exact-site
claim. This audit resolves the plan's “Laurion fading” note as an explicit
non-anchor, not as omitted research.

## Scope

The decision says nothing about the scale or continuity of slag reprocessing,
the dated history of every Laurion working, or later Roman/Late Roman activity.
It also does not make an inference about current in-game trade, which remains
separately blocked by the runtime RGO issue.

Sources: `PER-LAURION`; P12.1.
