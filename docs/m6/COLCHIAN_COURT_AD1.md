# Colchian court AD 1 roster slice

The British Museum records Antonia Tryphaena as the daughter of
Pythodorida/Pythodoris of Pontus and gives her life span as 10 BCE-AD 55. She
therefore demonstrably overlaps the campaign boundary (`BM-TRY` in the source
ledger). The existing Pythodoris record remains sourced to Strabo for Colchian
rule.

Antonia is represented only as a contested court-context member of Pythodoris's
Colchian household. The evidence does not establish her exact location on 1
January AD 1, so this is not a claim that she was physically resident at the
Colchian capital. It scripts no day-level biography, marriage, children, office,
future Thracian queenship, or succession. The shared engine dynasty records the
source's direct mother-daughter relation only and does not reconstruct a full
Pythodorid genealogy.
