# Peripheral courts at AD 1

This roster slice expands named court coverage without treating later reigns or
wars as AD 1 appointments. Its source keys are defined in the
[source ledger](../world_1ad/SOURCES.md).

## Eastern steppe

The later succession material in the *Han Shu* identifies Yu (later
Huduershidaogao Chanyu) and Yituzhiyashi as named members of the post-boundary
Xiongnu succession. Both therefore survived the campaign boundary. They remain
Xiongnu court identities under the existing Luandi engine label; neither is
given Wuzhuliu's current chanyu office, an heir slot, a dated biography, or a
reconstructed clan relationship. Source: `CTP-XIO`.

## Roman client courts

The Lexham scholarly synthesis calls Sampsigeramus II the probable son and
successor of Iamblichus II, while warning that the exact Emesene line of descent
is difficult. He is retained as a contested Emesan court identity only.

The Commagenean household remains intentionally unexpanded in this pass: the
available open academic passage securely identifies the earlier royal
generation but does not cleanly resolve an AD 1 court record for the later
Iotapa. Source: `LBD-EME`.

## Cheruscan elders

Tacitus's Germanicus narrative directly preserves Segestes and Inguiomerus
after the campaign boundary; Dio names Segimerus with Arminius among the AD 9
leaders. Their post-boundary activity establishes survival at AD 1. They remain
non-ruling `CRU` court identities: the anonymous Cheruscan government persists,
and Arminius's existing `ROM` service-context record is unchanged. Sources:
`TAC-CHER`; `DIO-CHER`.
