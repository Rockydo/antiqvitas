# Roman court AD 1 roster slice

This slice adds Lucius Caesar and Germanicus to the Julio-Claudian court
record. It deliberately leaves the government unchanged: Gaius Caesar remains
the one engine heir, Tiberius is neither adopted nor heir before AD 4, and no
one receives an invented military command or civil office.

## Evidence and limits

- Tacitus records that Augustus admitted Gaius and Lucius into the house of the
  Caesars and pursued their elevation as *principes iuventutis*.
  `TAC-LUC` in the source ledger supplies the primary-text route. Lucius is
  consequently a named dynastic court figure, not a second formal engine heir.
- The British Museum records Germanicus's 15 BCE-AD 19 lifetime and
  Julio-Claudian relationship (`BM-GER`). His addition supplies the plan's
  future Germanic-command figure without moving that command, Tiberius's AD 4
  adoption, or the Teutoburg sequence into the AD 1 start state.

Neither source provides a needed day-level biography date or an AD 1 office
assignment, so both fields remain blank. The M10 dated-history pass owns their
later careers.
