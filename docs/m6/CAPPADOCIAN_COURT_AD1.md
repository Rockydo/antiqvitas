# Cappadocian court scope at AD 1

Glaphyra is a bounded, living court figure rather than an engine consort or
officeholder. Josephus names her as King Archelaus of Cappadocia's daughter and
places her in the later tenth-year episode of Herod Archelaus's government.
That secures survival beyond the AD 1 boundary, but the source does not license
a campaign-start marriage, precise biography date, residence, office,
succession, or a fuller Cappadocian genealogy. Source: `JOS-GLA` in the source
ledger.

Tigranes V is a second, bounded Cappadocian court record. Josephus identifies
him as one of Glaphyra's sons, Oxford identifies him as a descendant of both
the Cappadocian and Herodian houses, and a numismatic collection dates his
later Armenian reign to AD 6-12. Those sources establish survival at the AD 1
boundary, not a campaign-start Armenian crown, claim, marriage, residence,
office, succession, or biography date. Sources: P8.1; `JOS-TIG`; `OCD-TIG5`;
`ANRO-TIG5` in the [source ledger](../world_1ad/SOURCES.md).
