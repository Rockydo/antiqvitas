# ANTIQVITAS Glossary

This is the project's controlled vocabulary for player-facing English. It
prefers a familiar ancient term where one is well established, while keeping
engine mechanics legible. A term describes its use in ANTIQVITAS; it is not a
claim that every historical society used the same institution in the same way.

Evidence codes point to the source catalogue in
[`world_1ad/SOURCES.md`](world_1ad/SOURCES.md). `P` refers to the relevant
section of the master plan. `†` marks a deliberately broad or contested
modelling label.

## Polities and government

| Term | Project usage | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Auctoritas** | The Roman legitimacy-facing language used for the Principate; it is not a separate constitutional office. | P13 |
| **Chanyu** | The title used for the leader of the Xiongnu confederation and its succession context. | BHR; P8.3, P13 |
| **Client kingdom** | A locally ruled polity bound to a larger power, represented by the `antq_client_kingdom` subject contract where the AD 1 relationship is sourced. | P8.1, P16.2 |
| **Dominate** | The late Roman imperial government path unlocked in the age beginning in AD 284; it does not imply a single, unchanging fourth-century constitution. | CAH-DIO; P13, P15 |
| **Foedus / foederati** | A late Roman land-for-service settlement relationship. The game term is unavailable before its AD 382 gate. | P15, P16.2 |
| **Ganasangha** | The project label for the historically attested Indian republican polities; it is not treated as a modern nation-state democracy. | P8.4, P13 |
| **Heqin** | Han–steppe marriage-and-peace diplomacy, used as contextual language rather than a claim that every frontier arrangement was identical. | BHR; P8.3 |
| **Imperium** | Roman command authority in flavor text; it is not interchangeable with a modern territorial state. | CAH-XI; P8.1 |
| **King of Kings** | The project-facing title for the Parthian royal hierarchy and its sub-king relationships. | P8.2, P13 |
| **Legate** | A Roman delegated commander or officeholder in flavor text. It is not automatically a campaign-start appointment. | CAH-XI; P8.1 |
| **Limes** | A frontier-defense system represented through roads, forts, and border mechanics, not a claim of one continuous wall. | P8.1, P12.3 |
| **Mandate of Heaven** | The Han legitimacy and dynastic-crisis mechanic; it is not a universal description of Chinese political thought. | BHR; P8.3, P13 |
| **Principate** | Rome's republic-facing imperial monarchy under Augustus, used for the first age and Roman government adapter. | MET; P8.1, P13, P15 |
| **Satrapy** | A Parthian subordinate-kingdom adapter, not a claim that every dependent polity had identical Achaemenid institutions. | P8.2, P16.2 |
| **Society of Pops (SoP)** | The game representation of a people without a settled-state tag. It is an engine model, never proof that a people lacked social or political organization. | P7.3 |
| **Tributary** | A country in the sourced Han or steppe tribute relationship represented by `antq_tributary`; it is not a blanket synonym for conquest. | P8.3, P16.2 |

## Religion, society, and culture

| Term | Project usage | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Imperial cult** | Roman religious-political practice expressed through the Religio Romana mechanics; it is not equated with Christianity. | P11, P13 |
| **Interpretatio** | The project label for cheap syncretic movement between compatible pagan pools; it simplifies varied ancient processes for a playable mechanic. | P11 |
| **Mystery cult** | An optional religious-aspect category for cults such as Isis, Mithras, Cybele, and Sol Invictus; it does not prescribe a uniform ritual experience. | P11 |
| **Orthodoxy** | A post-Nicaea doctrinal mechanic, not a retroactive label for all Christian communities before AD 325. | P11, P16.3 |
| **Peregrini** | Non-citizen inhabitants in the Roman citizenship ladder; the term is not applied outside its Roman legal context. | P13 |
| **Syncretic** | A neutral project descriptor for blended or overlapping religious practice. It never judges the authenticity of a community's worship. | P11 |
| **Tengri** | The game-facing steppe religious label; local traditions remain deliberately distinct rather than collapsed into one homogeneous culture. | P11 |

## Economy, war, and geography

| Term | Project usage | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Annona** | Rome's public grain-supply mechanism, represented by a market/building proxy rather than a reconstructed ration system. | P8.1, P12.3 |
| **Castra** | Roman military-fort language used for the frontier-fort adapter. | P12.3, P14 |
| **Cataphract** | Heavily armored cavalry represented as an Iranian and later Roman military advance; it is not a claim of identical equipment everywhere. | P14, P15 |
| **Known world** | A country-specific discovery horizon, not an assertion that populations had no knowledge beyond it. | P16.4 |
| **Monsoon route** | Indian-Ocean seasonal trade connectivity represented through markets, roads, and harbors rather than a scripted trade monopoly. | PER; P8.4, P12.2 |
| **Oikoumene** | The Roman/Greek world-horizon language used in flavor; it does not erase knowledge traditions outside the Mediterranean. | P16.4 |
| **RGO** | The EU5 resource-gathering output assigned to a location. It is an economic abstraction, not a measured ancient production total. | P12.1 |

## Time and historical currents

| Term | Project usage | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Age** | One of five campaign-era bands: Principate, High Empires, Crisis, Dominate, and Migrations. | P15 |
| **Historical current** | A dated situation, disaster, event, formation, or tag change that steers plausible history without forcing a single outcome. | P7.4, P17 |
| **Formation** | A new or transformed polity event used only where the project has a reviewed historical basis and a verified engine contract. | P17.3-17.4 |
| **Strong / Mild / Off** | The intended player rule for the weight of historical currents. It changes pressure, not the historical evidence behind a current. | P7.4 |
