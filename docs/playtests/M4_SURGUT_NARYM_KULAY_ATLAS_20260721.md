# M4 Surgut-Narym Ob Kulay atlas batch

Date: 2026-07-21

The thirty-seventh source-led M4 atlas batch assigns the installed Surgut,
Kargasok, and Tomsk provinces to a new `antq_kulay` culture. These three
selectors resolve twelve controlled locations in the Surgut-Narym/Ob zone.
Kulay is an archaeological label: it does not assert an ethnicity, language,
state, exact territorial boundary, or later Siberian identity.

The Tomsk Regional Museum places the culture's origin in the Surgut-Narym Ob
region and dates it from the fifth century BCE to the fifth century CE. The
scope is deliberately limited to the installed core provinces rather than the
source's broad statement about later Western-Siberian expansion.

The resulting ledger has 328 selectors, 12,030 resolved locations, 151 mapped
cultures, and a 172-culture M4 definition catalogue.

reported zero new `error.log` lines against the accepted baseline.
