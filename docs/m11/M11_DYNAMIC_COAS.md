# M11 scripted-formation standards

M10 creates or cosmetically transforms thirteen later polities. Their temporary
solid-color CoAs have been replaced with the same non-reconstructive,
emblem-on-field UI standard used for the AD 1 roster.

| Group | Tags | Motif family |
|---|---|---|
| Kushan / Xiongnu transformations | KSH, XSO, XNO | conch or horse, with distinct color fields |
| Champa formation | CPC | Southeast-Asian conch motif |
| Third-century formations | ALM, SAS, FRK | Germania boar or Iranian horse motif |
| Fourth-century transformations | HNS, WRE, ERO | steppe horse or Roman eagle motif |
| Final-century successors | VSG, VND, ODO | Germania boar or Roman eagle motif |

All thirteen emblems are installed generic colored-emblem assets. They are UI
standards, not reconstructions of battle flags, dynastic banners, ethnic
symbols, or specific material objects. The Huns, Roman successor identities,
and post-Roman kingdoms use broad functional motifs only.

The five owning M10 generators now produce these outputs directly.
`tools/m11_dynamic_coas.py --check` requires every tag, its reviewed emblem,
the full three-color contract, and the installed local asset to remain present.

Local basis: installed colored-emblem catalogue and CoA script contract. Design
basis: master plan sections 8, 17, and 20.
