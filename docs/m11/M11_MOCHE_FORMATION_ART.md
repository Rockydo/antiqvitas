# M11 Moche emergence illustration

The retained source is
`assets_queue/generated_sources/antq_moche_formation_source.png`; the 1080x440
master is `assets_queue/generated/antq_moche_formation_1080x440.png`; the
game-facing BC7 DDS is
`main_menu/gfx/interface/illustrations/event/antq_moche_formation.dds`.

The image-generation prompt requested a text-free, muted painterly north-coast
Peruvian river-valley context around AD 100: irrigation water, cultivated
patches, low generic adobe mounds, and hazed foothills. It forbade people,
armies, flags, writing, identifiable monuments, the later Chan Chan city, and
any claim of a unified Moche state. The result is therefore a regional
archaeological setting for an emergence current, not a reconstruction of a
capital, ruler, polity border, battle, or outcome.

`EVENT_IMAGES` in `tools/m10_second_century.py` owns the
`antq_m10_second.2001` link. The source/master/DDS chain is 1080x440 and was
visually inspected before validation and enabled-mod smoke.

Sources: ANTIQVITAS master plan sections 8.10, 9, 17.3, and 20; PNAS-VIR;
WIL-GAL; JSTOR-MOC; CAM-MOC; local EU5 country-event image contract.
